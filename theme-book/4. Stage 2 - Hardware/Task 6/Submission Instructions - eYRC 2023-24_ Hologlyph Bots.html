Video Recording Instructions

Each team creates a video for Task 6 of eYRC 2023-24. This video should comprise two parts: “Introduction Part” and “Demonstration Part”.

IMPORTANT: The purpose of the “Introduction Part” is to introduce the team and for us to understand your teams’ approach and method. The purpose of the “Demonstration Part” is for us to evaluate your Task 6 performance.

1. Introduction Part

The video should start with a brief introduction. Please follow the instructions below:

  • Opening screen of the video should show the Team ID, College name, Theme name and Team members’ names and should remain for 3-5 seconds. You can refer to the “Title_slide ” provided.
  • This should be followed by an introduction limited to a maximum of TWO minutes:
    • A short introduction by each team member where they can mention their names and department in the college. (30 seconds)
    • A maximum 30 seconds introduction by the team leader highlighting any interesting aspect of the theme’s implementation.
    • A maximum 60 seconds description highlighting the challenges faced while completing the task.

The team may also answer the following questions conforming to the time limit:

  1. What were the major learnings and impact of the theme?
  2. You can explain how this Theme and eYRC as a whole impacted your learnings.*
  3. How did the team divide the work?
  4. Be honest on how the team has divided various tasks during the course of 6-7 months.*
  5. What were the major challenges faced by the team?
  6. Team can cover a couple of challenges and how they tackled them.*

NOTE: Both video and audio should be clearly visible and audible for all the team members.

2. Demonstration part

  1. The arena/environment must conform to the Original/Bonus Configuration (as available on the portal) of Task 6 before you start shooting/recording the video.
  2. Demonstration part MUST be a one-shot continuous video and it should not be edited in terms of being fast forwarded or slowed down, the run time should be the same as actual run time. Teams uploading an edited video will be disqualified from the competition.
  3. Team should add a countdown video at the start of the Demonstration Part. You may use the following youtube video: Countdown Video for countdown.
  4. Theme Video should NOT contain any visual and/or audio noise before Theme Run Start Time and after Theme Run End Time.
  5. During the Demonstration Part, you must add a pre-recorded voice-over/narration. This would ensure that you show your originality and highlight your hard-work and also the video does not sound bland.

A nice and clear narration would get your point across to the technical team. This should be a natural dialogue flow which will ensure that the judges as well as the viewers are in sync with what the robot/drone is doing during the run.

NOTE: There should be No video transition/s or background music in this part and simply a voice-over/narration along with the Demonstration Part is needed.

4. Instructions for Recording Video

4.1. Hardware and Assembly

  • The recommended resolution of the camera should be 8 Megapixels (MP) or higher.
  • The camera should be mounted and not hand-held, this is to avoid shakes and blurs in the video while recording.

4.2. Continuity and Merging

  • The video you submit will have 2 segments as described above: Introduction Part and Demonstration Part.
  • Each of the two segments must be one continuous shot, i.e. it should not have discontinuities within them. The only discontinuity allowed in your final video submission is between the video of your Introduction and the video of your theme run.
  • Merging two segments: Once you have these two videos corresponding to each segment, each of them shot continuously, without any discontinuity within, it’s time to merge them. Put a countdown timer as mentioned in the Demonstration Part above. You can use any video editing application to do the same. The figure below describes the process in detail.

4.3. Sound and Lighting

  • The video should be recorded in a quiet and a well-lit environment.
  • There should be no interference in terms of background noise or movement while shooting the video.
  • Keep the lighting conditions steady throughout the video. Sudden change in light might falsely lead one to believe editing of the video and hence, disqualification.
  • Avoid specular reflection as much as possible on any part of the frame/arena. For reference, check the figure below. The image on the left has specular reflection which is highlighted by the colour green on the image on the right.

4.4. Theme run recording The following windows need to be recorded simultaneously for the submission:

  • Evaluator terminal(Controller terminal for function mode). All the data(score,runtime) should be visible in the terminal for image mode.
  • Overhead camera feed
  • Actual bot in action

4.5. Camera settings:

  • Select the highest resolution / HD resolution in your camera / mobile settings (at least 8MPs).
  • Ensure the sound quality is crystal clear in both videos. Avoid extraneous audio/visual noise in the recording place.
  • Hold the camera / mobile phone horizontally. Do NOT shoot in vertical framing/shorts/reel style.
  • Ensure that the mobile is mounted on a tripod / stand / selfie stick so that there is no/minimum movement while shooting.
  • While speaking during the Introduction Part, look into the camera and speak. Do not look elsewhere.
  • Ensure there is adequate lighting. Your face should be seen clearly.
  • Avoid a situation with a light in the background as the face will be in silhouette.
  • Don’t keep the camera too close, else it distorts the subject - ideally keep it 4-5 feet away.

4.6. Background settings:

  • Choose a quiet place in your house. Kindly ensure that there is no noise in the background.
  • Ensure that the background is clutter free. (no bed / table / people / chairs are seen in the background)
  • Preferably select a plain wall as background.
  • Wear contrasting coloured clothes against your selected background. Do not wear striped shirts / kurtas). Big prints / plain coloured clothes will do.
  • Switch off the AC and if possible switch off, preferably even the fan (if noisy) while shooting else there will be lots of disturbance.

5. General Instructions:

  • The team needs to ensure that the video flow and edit is crisp.
  • Introduction Part MUST NOT EXCEED 2 MINS so keep it short and sweet.
  • The Demonstration Part should NOT contain any external audio/video noise apart from the narration/voiceover as explained above.
  • We know you all are quite creative, but it would be great if you refrain from using any flashy transitions or graphics in this video specially in the Demonstration Part.

6. Submission Instructions:

6.1 Video Submission

  • Merge the Introduction and the Demonstration Video together and upload the video to Youtube and name the video - eYRC-HB#_Task6_Original/Bonus_Configuration. Additionally as done in Task 5B merge photos of the final drawing at the end of the video. Also share these photos in the code submission.

    • For example, for theme HB with team ID 9999, then your video will be titled eYRC-HB#9999_Task6_Original_Configuration for Original Configuration Submission.
    • Make sure the video is uploaded as unlisted.

  • Your respective Theme Developers will be contacting you for further submission instructions.

6.2 Code Submission

Once you are satisfied with your results, create a zip with following structure(similar to task 5B):

HB#TeamID_Task6.zip

  • Task6_original_grader_output.txt
  • Task6_bonus_grader_output.txt
  • controller.py
  • feedback.py
  • Photos //Add photos of the final drawings and the bots in this folder.

Note: If you have other relevant files, upload all of them with proper documentation.

ALL THE BEST and see you in the eYRC Grand Finale !!!!
